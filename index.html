JEE Preparation Data Story

Preparing for the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) is no small feat, and the journey is different for every student. This study dives into the stories of college students who are navigating the JEE prep maze, shedding light on some interesting choices along the way. How early did they start? Which coaching institute did they trust with their future? And, the burning question—did they choose to take a "drop year" to level up their preparation? By exploring these factors, this data reveals the strategies and trends that shape the path to success (or struggle) in one of the most competitive exams in India. Let’s take a closer look at how students are gearing up for the big day, from starting in school to making pivotal decisions about their study plans!

Kickstarting the JEE prep journey

The timing of when students start their JEE preparation can make all the difference in their approach and mindset. Some dive into the grind as early as high school, while others wait until later, feeling the pressure to catch up. This section explores the pivotal moment when students decided to begin their journey, whether it was in their early teenage years, during school, or after graduation. Understanding these choices helps us grasp how preparation timelines impact their overall strategy and success in conquering the JEE .

Here we can observe that majority students started preparation from class 11.

Which Coaching to trust

With so many coaching institutes offering JEE prep courses, it can be overwhelming for students to choose the right one. Whether it’s renowned national names or local institutes with personalized attention, the decision can significantly influence a student’s preparation journey.

While big names like FIITJEE, ALLEN are popular choices, many students prefer other local options.

The decision to opt for a drop year

For many JEE aspirants, the choice to take a drop year is a major turning point. Some see it as a second chance to improve their rank, while others hesitate due to the risks involved. From the pie chart, we can infer that around 75% students didn't opt for a drop year.

Does starting late have its impacts

The timing of JEE preparation often plays a crucial role in a student’s journey. Some who start early build a steady foundation, while late starters may find themselves rushing to cover the vast syllabus. This raises an important question—does starting late increase the chances of taking a drop year?

Conclusion

Final thoughts and takeaways.

  • Class 11 is the most preferred for students to kickstart the prep. One of the reason for this could be that at this class students have finally opted for the desired stream and have a better clarity about their career options
  • Local coaching options are most preferred by students as they are much cheaper than big coaching institutes like FIITJEE,ALLEN.
  • Dropping out is a big decision and around 75% students decided to opt out.
  • The last plot shows that majority students who opted for drop started their preparation from 12th. Starting late compared to their peers can have a negative effect as they would have to complete the vast syllabus in a short time. While there could also be other reasons, example in the case of student who started preparation from 9th opted from drop because of medical reasons.
